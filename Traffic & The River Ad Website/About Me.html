Hello there, I’m Gregory Desrosiers. I’m 18 years old and a student of Champlain College St-Lambert. I’m studying Computer Science in becoming a computer game and site designer.

Ever since my childhood, I’ve been occupied mostly with electronics that you would normally see what’s happening with the system on the TV or the monitor. However, after spending a few years working with a few social workers in an attempt for me to be adapted to more freedom, I decided to become more sociable. One of the key breakthroughs was when in Summer 2009, I started having my own Facebook account. Today, I still use it a lot, because it would be the cure for social dependency, where if I wasn’t on Facebook, I would feel like I’m not integrated to society enough.

The thing about becoming a game designer is, if you’re trying to get used to an artificial world and there’s a story on top of it, you should go through it to explore the world itself. Of course, a world isn’t complete with scenery, terrain, characters, and of course, what happens. What matters is you put every single detail in the right way, and make sure it flows. If you have a glitch in the game, it distorts the world and the feeling becomes destroyed. Becoming a Web designer also sometimes has a fundamental artificial world where the site is like a castle and the pages you enter are the rooms. The Contact Us page would become the throne room, where the king or queen has the power. That would be the person who’s in charge of the whole castle, which would be like me, as Web Producer & Director.

I was diagnosed with Asperger’s syndrome when I was two and I began going through a lot of stressful stuff that I wanted to keep to myself, be so self-centered and not adapt to the real world. But sometime during high school, I began adapting up to the level where I’m much closer to being like everyone else at the level of society.

The reason why I’m making Traffic & The River is the fact that in Summer 2011, I was not able to get a job. Not because the market was closed, but because I had a preferred salary on my CV. I wanted to make $15 / hr because I wanted to save money in my debit account for setting up a company to develop video games and develop & publish online games on what may become our Web site. It’s the economic inflation that makes me want to invest a lot instead of sticking with the usual $10/hr or minimal wage. By the time I graduate from university, even if I make a business plan, I may need a capital investment of a lot more than $2 million to hire the required software engineers, Web engineers, artists and all other important positions. I’ll have to hire more than 200 employees to meet the demands of today’s Internet and video game markets.

Another reason why I’m doing it is because besides helping me get out of a situation where I’m feeling too disadvantaged, I also want to spend a couple of weeks in New York City either as a vacation or both vacation and marketing my music videos.

Although I do want to get together with my friends, the trouble is, whenever they want to spend time together, they don’t want to invite me. However, as I was doing my first semester of college, my English teacher, Ms. Norcene Webb, noticed that I’m having trouble with language skills, and actually, it’s true. There are so many vocabulary words, clichés, and expressions that I am not aware of where if I had more of a linguistic sense, then I can explain my stuff more coherently as well as develop more of my interest to friends by exploring more of what they like. I even tried out a book that Barbara Goodall (one of the technicians at Heritage Regional High School’s Student Services) gave me in which it contained idioms, but it was nearly impossible for me to keep the meaning and the words together in my head. I’m more of a person who is productive at something instead of going farther in terms of language skills. Still, I’m doing my best to get help on language skills.

New York City has its own world to share with the people on planet Earth. It’s even going to share with me! I rather be in cities that have things like LED and LCD billboards on buildings and feel like you’re in the middle of a city with so much to do. I really want to spend my time checking out the major highlights, including the Empire State Building, Times Square, Central Park, Brooklyn Bridge, Statue of Liberty, and the World Trade Center Memorial. I want to listen to “Empire State of Mind” by Jay-Z and Alicia Keys as I explore and even spend some time on the beaches checking out the amusements, the fresh Atlantic Ocean, and of course, the arcade parlors. All I need to do is enjoy the ride, on the NYC Subway, to Long Island. Besides, I can’t wait to enjoy the fabulous breakfasts I would deserve to pay and eat over there in the city of my dreams. Best of all, I want to be engaged socially, which means I want to encourage my friends who knew me for long to come to New York and spend the vacation with me. They can even be engaged with me to sell the pack to New Yorkers who want to explore more about me and want to see it. There’s so many to do in New York, it’s like you want to live over there to feel like you are really part of the world, in a more recognizable fashion.

So, let’s see if my dream will really come true! If you’re looking for a recognizable man who will want to create a video game with Nintendo’s Princess Daisy, please consider me!
